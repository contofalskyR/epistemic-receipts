# Epistemic Receipts — Legal Pre-Launch Checklist

*This checklist is ordered by dependencies — items earlier in the list unlock items later. Owner must complete or delegate each item.*

---

## Phase 0 — Before any public or commercial commitment

### ☐ 0.1 Form a legal entity (before any paid contract)

**Rationale:** Operating as an individual creates personal liability for any contract you sign or dispute that arises from the site. An LLC (or equivalent) created before the first commercial licence agreement limits that liability.

**Action:**
- Decide jurisdiction (see item 1.2).
- File an LLC or other entity. In the US, Delaware or your home state are the common choices. Delaware is preferred for investment-ready structures; home state is simpler and cheaper if you are not raising.
- Register a DBA ("Epistemic Receipts") if the entity name differs.
- Open a business bank account after formation.

**Blocks:** Everything in Phase 1.

---

### ☐ 0.2 Trademark search — "Epistemic Receipts"

**Rationale:** Before filing or launching publicly, confirm that "Epistemic Receipts" is not already registered in your class(es). A conflicting registration could force a rebrand.

**Action:**
- Run a USPTO TESS search at tess2.uspto.gov for "Epistemic Receipts" in IC 042 (software/database services) and IC 041 (educational services).
- If clear, consider filing a use-based or intent-to-use trademark application.
- International: check WIPO Madrid system if non-US markets matter.

**Blocks:** Public launch, any branding investment.

## Phase 1 — Counsel review (engage a technology/data attorney)

### ☐ 1.1 Counsel review of all legal drafts

**Rationale:** All files in `legal/` are clearly marked DRAFT and were generated by an AI agent, not an attorney. Every document requires attorney review before public use.

**Documents to review (in order):**
1. `legal/LICENSE-community.md` — confirm ODC-By–style structure is appropriate; confirm "no training use" clause is enforceable; check if "substantial part" needs definition.
2. `legal/LICENSE-commercial.md` — fill in all `[PLACEHOLDER]` fields; confirm term sheet reflects your actual commercial intent; have counsel draft a full agreement template based on this skeleton.
3. `legal/terms-of-service.md` — fill jurisdiction, limitation of liability cap, dispute resolution mechanism.
4. `legal/privacy-policy.md` — confirm GDPR/CCPA applicability; confirm legal basis for each processing activity; add DSR (data subject rights) procedure; confirm Resend and Vercel data processing agreements are in place.

**Blocks:** Public launch of `/license`, `/terms`, `/privacy` pages; any commercial conversation.

---

### ☐ 1.2 Jurisdiction decision

**Rationale:** Where the entity is formed and where the Terms of Service say disputes will be resolved are separate decisions. You need both before the ToS goes live.

**Action:**
- Decide entity jurisdiction (typically where you reside or Delaware for US entities).
- Decide governing law for contracts (US-based customers: your state or Delaware; EU customers: counsel may recommend EU-friendly alternatives).
- Decide dispute resolution: arbitration (JAMS/AAA), small claims, or courts.

**Blocks:** Finalising ToS; finalising any commercial licence.

---

### ☐ 1.3 Data processing agreements

**Rationale:** If users from the EU subscribe to topic alerts, GDPR requires a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with each sub-processor that handles personal data.

**Action:**
- Verify Resend's DPA is in place (resend.com provides a standard DPA). Sign if required.
- Verify Vercel's DPA is in place (vercel.com provides a standard DPA). Sign if required.
- If adding additional processors (e.g., analytics, CRM), repeat for each.

**Blocks:** EU user onboarding; GDPR compliance.

---

## Phase 2 — Upstream dataset redistribution review

### ☐ 2.1 Audit every pipeline's upstream terms

**Rationale:** The snapshot export feature (Spec 12) and any commercial licence that grants redistribution rights can only legally include data that upstream providers permit to be redistributed. Some pipelines have unclear or restrictive terms.

**Action:**
- Review `legal/upstream-licenses.md` — the full table of pipeline upstream terms.
- For every row marked **UNKNOWN**: research the actual terms before including that pipeline's data in any snapshot export.
- For every row marked **REDISTRIBUTION: No** or **RESTRICTED**: exclude that pipeline from snapshot exports until redistribution rights are confirmed or a separate agreement is reached with the upstream provider.
- Specific pipelines to prioritise (flagged in `upstream-licenses.md`):
  - `omim_v1` — OMIM has a licence that restricts redistribution; verify and resolve before snapshots.
  - `retraction_watch_v1` — Retraction Watch terms are unclear; verify.
  - `stasi_v1` — Bundesarchiv access terms; verify.
  - `nato_official_texts_v1` — NATO CPS terms; verify before full ingest run.
  - Any pipeline with `UNKNOWN` upstream terms.

**Blocks:** Spec 12 snapshot exports containing those pipelines; any commercial redistribution licence.

---

### ☐ 2.2 CCES / ConstituentOpinion upstream terms

**Rationale:** If any data from the Cooperative Congressional Election Study (CCES) or ConstituentOpinion datasets has been or will be ingested, their redistribution terms must be confirmed before snapshot inclusion.

**Action:**
- Confirm whether CCES data is currently in the database (search pipeline tags for any CCES reference).
- If yes, review CCES redistribution terms at the Harvard Dataverse entry.
- Repeat for ConstituentOpinion or any other survey dataset.

---

### ☐ 2.3 European parliamentary dataset terms

**Rationale:** `riksdag_v1`, `tweedekamer_v1`, `bundestag_v1`, `nationalrat_v1`, `oireachtas_v1`, and `canada_bills_v1` all use open government APIs, but their redistribution terms vary.

**Action:**
- Confirm open-government licence for each (see `upstream-licenses.md`).
- Where terms require attribution, ensure attribution is included in snapshot manifests.

## Phase 3 — Before first commercial agreement

### ☐ 3.1 Have counsel draft the full commercial licence agreement

**Rationale:** `legal/LICENSE-commercial.md` is a skeleton term sheet, not an executable contract.

**Action:**
- Brief counsel on the term sheet; have them draft a full commercial licence agreement.
- Decide which tier(s) to offer first and what pricing to set.
- Prepare a short-form click-through version for lower-value API customers.

---

### ☐ 3.2 Set up billing infrastructure

**Rationale:** Before accepting payment, you need a payment processor and invoicing.
- Decide: Stripe, invoice-only, or another payment method.
- Set up business bank account (see 0.1).

---

## Phase 4 — Ongoing

### ☐ 4.1 Annual legal review

Review and update all legal documents at least annually, or whenever:
- A major new pipeline is added with non-standard upstream terms.
- A new jurisdiction's users account for meaningful traffic (triggers privacy law review).
- The Terms of Service or data practices change materially.

### ☐ 4.2 Upstream licence monitoring

When a new pipeline is added, add a row to `legal/upstream-licenses.md` before the pipeline is included in any snapshot export.
