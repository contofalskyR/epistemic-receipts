## The 5 candidates

**Candidate 1 — The existing headline (safe default)**
> How long does "settled" stay settled?

Current h1. Clean, accurate, and already in production. Retain if no other candidate lands better.

**Candidate 2 — Receipt framing**
> Knowledge changes. We keep the receipts.

Establishes the site name, frames the epistemic-receipt metaphor, and implies the archive/provenance angle. Risk: "receipts" needs context to land — clearest if seen alongside the corpus numbers.

**Candidate 3 — The adversarial provocation**
> The science was settled. Then it wasn't.

Punchy, implies reversal, creates immediate tension. Best for users arriving from skeptical angles (misinformation research, policy). Risk: reads slightly cynical — could attract bad-faith readers who want "science is wrong."

**Candidate 4 — The temporal hook**
> Every claim has a birthday. Some have a death date.

More evocative; makes the dated-trajectory concept visceral. Works well with the settling-curve figure directly below. Risk: "death date" may read morbid; "birthday" can read colloquial.

**Candidate 5 — The observatory framing**
> A research observatory of claims, aging in public.

Matches the meta description ("research observatory") and establishes the observational-not-adversarial tone. Risk: "aging in public" is novel phrasing that needs a beat to parse.
