**Tiering logic:**  
Tier 1 requires one community's latest axis ∈ {SETTLED, REVERSED} AND a different community's latest axis ∈ {CONTESTED, REVERSED, ABANDONED}, with the two values being different (incompatible endpoints). Everything else in the divergent bucket is Tier 2. Defined in `scripts/b4-divergence-tiers.ts` and shared via `lib/split-ledger.ts`.

## Anomalies noted (data, not errors)

1. **REVERSED vs RECORDED in Tier 2:** Many Tier-2 samples show EXPERT_LITERATURE=RECORDED alongside INSTITUTIONAL=REVERSED. These are drug retraction cases where the literature has the publication as a reference record but the FDA has withdrawn the approval. Per the brief's Tier definition, REVERSED vs RECORDED is stage-lag (RECORDED is not in the Tier-1 "other side" list: {CONTESTED, REVERSED, ABANDONED}). This is correct behavior — listing as a potential anomaly for owner awareness. No action taken.

2. **312 of 386 Tier-1 claims are curated (81%):** Higher than expected. The curated `trajectory:` prefix spans pharmaceutical drug labels from a join with curated trajectories. Worth investigating whether the curated-vs-pipeline split is meaningful for display purposes. No page changes made.

3. **Pre-existing ESLint errors in `/reversals/page.tsx`:** Unescaped `"` at line 292 — "debunked". Pre-existing before B4 (confirmed by stash check). Not introduced by B4.
